by Saria Mostafa

Nebulight's Centralized Open-Source Physics Simulation Project is redefining scientific exploration by offering a comprehensive platform for researchers and educators worldwide. At its core, Nebulight's project boasts a scalable infrastructure, leveraging cloud-based resources and modular design principles to provide users with unparalleled flexibility and control over their experiments.

Low-Budget, Commercially Viable Cosmic Waves Detector

Everyone can be a scientist

At Nebulight, we're excited to introduce our latest project in development: the low-budget, commercially viable cosmic waves detector. As we continue to refine and perfect this groundbreaking device, we're committed to pushing the boundaries of cosmic exploration while maintaining affordability.

Our cosmic waves detector is designed with the user in mind. We've prioritized intuitive controls and a user-friendly interface, ensuring that researchers and enthusiasts of all skill levels can easily operate the device. Whether you're a seasoned astrophysicist or a curious amateur astronomer, our detector offers a hassle-free experience, allowing you to focus on the excitement of cosmic exploration without worrying about complex technicalities.

One of the most exciting features of our cosmic waves detector is its ability to collect data from diverse locations around the world. By being posted in many different places across the globe, our detector can capture a wide range of cosmic phenomena, providing researchers with valuable insights into the universe's mysteries. This diversity in data collection ensures that our detector offers a more comprehensive understanding of cosmic waves than traditional detectors, which are often limited to specific locations or regions.

How we started

Ole Rieken and Saria Mostafa

We have always been fascinated by the natural sciences, even becoming "Juniorstudenten" at the university of Hamburg (UHH).

Yet, it has always been the case that in science, there are very few things you can do on your own. So, getting together a team of passionate and well-versed people together, to learn and push the boundary of what is possible seemed to be the most logical course of action.

Thus, we started Nebulight. To accomplish exactly what we have always wanted, getting a group of cool people together to create and learn together, to push our limits, to become the future of science.

Repurposing old hardware

by Nebulight

At Nebulight, we're committed to pushing the boundaries of innovation while also minimizing our environmental footprint. That's why we're proud to share our approach to repurposing used, broken, and damaged hardware to build upon our equipment, contributing to a more sustainable future.

Rather than discarding hardware that is no longer functional or deemed obsolete, we see potential in every piece of equipment. Join us and donate your hardware by contacting us! Through careful assessment and refurbishment, our team of engineers and technicians works tirelessly to salvage and repurpose hardware components, breathing new life into old technology.

Repurposing hardware offers a multitude of benefits. Not only does it reduce electronic waste and lessen the strain on natural resources, but it also allows us to save costs and extend the lifespan of our equipment. Additionally, by repurposing hardware, we're able to tap into a diverse range of components and materials, fostering creativity and innovation in our projects.

A New Microprocessor Architecture for AI

by Saria Mostafa

Unlike traditional microprocessor architectures, which are often designed with general-purpose computing tasks in mind, our new architecture is optimized specifically for the demands of AI workloads. By incorporating specialized hardware accelerators and optimized instruction sets, we're able to dramatically improve the performance and efficiency of AI algorithms, leading to faster training times and more accurate predictions.

With the rapid advancement of AI technology, there's an increasing demand for more powerful and efficient hardware solutions. Our new microprocessor architecture aims to meet this demand head-on, providing researchers and developers with the tools they need to push the boundaries of what's possible in AI research and development. Whether it's training neural networks, processing natural language, or analyzing complex data sets, our architecture is designed to excel in a wide range of AI applications.
